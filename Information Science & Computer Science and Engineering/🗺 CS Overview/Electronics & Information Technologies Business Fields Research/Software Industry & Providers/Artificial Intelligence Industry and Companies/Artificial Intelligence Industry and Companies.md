### Layers In AI Industry
#### U.S. Led Western

**Layer 1: The Infrastructure (The "Power Grid")**
This layer provides the physical "muscle" required to train and run models. It is the most capital-intensive part of the industry.

|**Sub-Layer**|**Key Players**|**What they are doing**|
|---|---|---|
|**Chips (GPUs/LPUs)**|**NVIDIA, AMD, Groq, Intel**|NVIDIA (Rubin architecture) dominates training; Groq competes on ultra-fast inference; AMD is the primary "open" hardware alternative.|
|**Cloud (Hyperscalers)**|**AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud**|Buying hundreds of thousands of chips to rent them back to model makers. They are also building "Sovereign Clouds" for national data security.|
|**Data Centers**|**CoreWeave, Lambda Labs, Equinix**|"Alternative Hyperscalers" that focus specifically on high-density GPU clusters for AI, often outperforming the big three in specialized speed.|


**Layer 2: The Model Layer (The "Brains")**
This layer involves the research labs creating the actual Large Language Models (LLMs). This has split into two distinct philosophies: **Frontier** and **Specialized**.
- **Frontier Labs (The Giants):**  
	- **OpenAI (GPT-5/o2):** Shifting toward "Reasoning" models and multi-modal integration.
    - **Anthropic (Claude 3.5/4):** Focusing on "Constitutional AI" (safety) and massive 200k+ context windows for enterprise.
    - **Google (Gemini):** Dominating multi-modality by natively processing video, audio, and text in one stream.
- **Open-Source/Open-Weight Leaders:** 
	- Qwan
	- DeepSeek
	- **Meta (Llama 4):** Providing the "operating system" for the rest of the world.
    - **Mistral:** Developing "Mixture of Experts" (MoE) models that are highly efficient and cheap to run.
- **Specialized Models:** Companies like **DeepSeek** (coding/math) or **Harvey** (legal) that create models specifically tuned for one industry.


**Layer 3: The Mid-Stack / Inference (The "Distribution")**
This is the most technical layer, focused on making models **fast, cheap, and accessible.**
- **Inference Providers:** 
	- **Fireworks.ai, Together AI, and Groq Cloud.** They take the models from Layer 2 and run them on the hardware from Layer 1. They compete on "tokens per second."
- **Aggregators:** 
	- **OpenRouter.** A single API that lets you swap between any of the models above instantly.
- **Developer Frameworks:** 
	- **LangChain, LlamaIndex, and Vercel AI SDK.** These are the toolkits engineers use to "plumb" AI into actual websites and apps.


**Layer 4: The Application & Agent Layer (The "Value")**
This is the layer users actually interact with. The trend in 2026 has moved from "Chatbots" to **"Agents."**
- **Enterprise Agents:** **Salesforce (Agentforce), Microsoft (Copilot Studio), and ServiceNow.** These companies build agents that don't just "talk" but actually _do_ workâ€”like filing an insurance claim or updating a CRM autonomously.
- **Vertical AI:** Companies building AI for a specific niche.
    - **Healthcare:** **Neko Health** (AI diagnostics).
    - **Video/Media:** **Synthesia** (AI avatars) and **Runway** (generative video).
- **Consumer AI Hardware:** **OpenAI/Apple partnership, Humane, and Rabbit.** Devices designed to let you interact with AI without looking at a traditional screen.


Summary of the "2026 Shift"
The biggest change this year is the **"Great Decoupling."** Companies are moving away from using one giant model (like GPT-4) for everything. Instead, they use:
1. **Large Frontier Models** for complex reasoning and planning.
2. **Small Language Models (SLMs)** for fast, on-device, or cheap routine tasks.
3. **Inference Engines** to orchestrate them all together.
