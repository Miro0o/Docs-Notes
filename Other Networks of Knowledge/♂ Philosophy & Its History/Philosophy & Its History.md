[Western philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_philosophy "Western philosophy")Â originated with anÂ [inquiry into the fundamental nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphysics "Metaphysics")Â of the cosmos inÂ [Ancient Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece"). TheÂ [medieval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval "Medieval")Â period was focused more onÂ [theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theology "Theology"). The modern period was characterized by an increased focus on how philosophical and scientific knowledge is created. Its new ideas were used during theÂ [Enlightenment period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment "Age of Enlightenment")Â to challenge traditional authorities. Influential developments in the 19th and 20th centuries includedÂ [German idealism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_idealism "German idealism"),Â [pragmatism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pragmatism "Pragmatism"),Â [positivism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Positivism "Positivism"),Â [formal logic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formal_logic "Formal logic"), linguistic analysis,Â [phenomenology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenomenology_\(philosophy\) "Phenomenology (philosophy)"),Â [existentialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existentialism "Existentialism"), andÂ [postmodernism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postmodernism "Postmodernism").

[Indian philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_philosophy "Indian philosophy")Â is characterized by its combined interest in the nature of reality, the ways of arriving at knowledge, and theÂ [spiritual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spirituality "Spirituality")Â question of how to reachÂ [enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enlightenment_in_Buddhism "Enlightenment in Buddhism"). In the modern period, the exchange between Indian and Western thought led various Indian philosophers to develop comprehensive systems. They aimed to unite and harmonize diverse philosophical and religious schools of thought.

Central topics inÂ [Chinese philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_philosophy "Chinese philosophy")Â were right social conduct, government, andÂ [self-cultivation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-cultivation "Self-cultivation").
